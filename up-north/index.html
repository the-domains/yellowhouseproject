Up North

For as long as I can remember, my grandparents took me to the cabin up north. That’s what we called it, ‘Up North’. I loved going, sometimes with one of my brothers or sisters or just with my grandparents. It was an escape to another time and place; a one room cabin deep in the woods with no one around. Did I mention, there was no electricity or running water or toilet. Even growing up before I-pads and cell phones, getting away to an oil lamp, rustic, cabin was bliss. For my mom and siblings, this was where we learned to hike to get water from a spring, chop wood and make a fire in the stove, use an out-house, have family time and to entertain ourselves. I still appreciate it and go Up North with friends to get away and play gin-rummy and dominoes. Those great memories and relaxing times with my Grandparents, Mom, siblings and my daughter will always stay with me.
